René Laurent Bourdet

René was born in San Francisco, California in 1920. He was raised in a two-parent home at 1562 Bush Street in San Francisco, His father, Cyprien owned a French laundry. His mother, Lucie also worked there. His older brother, Pierre (Pete) worked at a bakery until he enlisted in the Army during World War II. They lived over the top of their laundry (which is still there [see Google Map]).

The family was Catholic, and they worshipped at Ã‰glise Notre-Dame-des-Victoires, also on Bush Street. During High School, René attended High School of Commerce on Van Ness Avenue where he excelled in Basketball.

Around 1937-38, the family moved to Saratoga, California, which at that time was a famous fruit growing area now known as "Silicon Valley". René's father bought a bunch of land with other French Americans, and created Wildwood Park, a resort and amusement park for the small community.

René graduated from Los Gatos High School, and shortly after started working as a butcher at Foothill Meat Market in Saratoga.

He was drafted in 1942 to serve in the US Navy. He was stationed in the New York Naval yard where he helped translate for the French ships coming in to port. In February of 1943, he joined the crew of the USS Laub, DD 613, a Benson class destroyer where he served as ship's cook.

Upon his return, he married Connie Gilbert and had two daughters, Linda, born in 1945, and Elaine born in 1947. At some point he and Connie separated, and he met and married Lydia Anne Stewart of Ithaca, New York.
